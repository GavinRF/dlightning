You have an idea. Maybe it's a solution to a problem you've personally experienced, or something you've noticed others struggling with. You can picture the product, imagine how people will use it, and feel excited about bringing it to life.

But before you invest months and resources into development, there's a crucial question to answer: Will people actually want what you're planning to build?

When they open your product for the first time each day, what do they see?

Maybe it's the feature that took months to build but somehow falls flat. Or the onboarding flow that made perfect sense in the wireframes but creates friction in practice. Perhaps it's the interface element that your team loves, but users skip entirely.

Gaps between intention and experience aren't design failures. They're signals. Your product is trying to tell you something about the distance between what you built and what people actually need.
